What is Educational Accreditation?




Welcome to the 4Z-Pedia. Many countries consider education as a part of its national security. Education is what raises the leaders of the society, its scholars, protectors, teachers, doctors, engineers, etc. So, let's learn more about accreditation of educational institutions, that ensures quality education for every individual in the society



Quality Education:

  • A quality education is one that focuses on the whole student; the social, emotional, mental, physical, & cognitive development of each student regardless of gender, race, ethnicity, socioeconomic status, or geographic location.
  • A quality education provides resources & directs policy to ensure that each student enters schooln or university healthy & learns about & practices a healthy lifestyle; learns in an environment that is physically & emotionally safe for students & teachers; is actively engaged in learning & is connected to the school or university & broader community; has access to personalized learning & is supported by qualified, caring teachers; & is challenged academically & prepared for success in college or further study & for employment & participation in a global environment.
  • A quality education provides the outcomes needed for individuals, communities, & societies to prosper.
  • A quality education allows schools or universities to align & integrate fully with their communities & access a range of services across sectors designed to support the educational development of their students.
  • A quality education is supported by 3 key pillars: ensuring access to quality teachers; providing use of quality learning tools & professional development; & the establishment of safe & supportive quality learning environments.
  • A quality education should prepare students for LIFE, with all its current complexities, not just prepare them for EXAMS.

Educational Accreditation:

  • Educational accreditation is the process of evaluation & verification of the quality of services & operations of any educational institution, or program by an external body. In order to determine whether applicable & recognized standards are met. If standards are fulfilled, accreditation is granted.
  • To be accredited, schools, institutes or universities must undertake an extensive process that requires them to successfully pass quality indicators as: manage accurate data, design high-standard courses, carry out periodical assessments to evaluate & enhance performance of students, provide top-level facilities, teaching aids, etc.
  • Educational accreditation is performed in most countries by a governmental organization, usually the ministry of education.
  • In USA, the government delegates instead the accreditation to private, non-profit organizations (accreditors), all accreditors in USA must in turn be recognized by the national advisory committee on institutional quality & integrity (NACIQI), which is a body of the U.S. ministry of education. That means that also in the US, accreditation is indirectly controlled by the government.



Purposes of the Accreditation:

  1. Academic Quality: standards have been designed to enhance academic quality in schools, institutes & universities. Standards emphasize student achievement & high expectations of teaching, learning, research, service, & outreach.
  2. Accountability: educational institutions seeking accreditation have accountability standards that are consistent, clear, & communicated to the public & the community. Results of educational outcomes are readily available & used in planning for change & enhancing the program.
  3. Planning & Improvement: accredited institutions are encouraged to plan, where needed, for change & for improving the learning environment. institutions anticipate & address change in a timely & methodical manner. Student achievement is stressed.



Who benefits from the Accreditation?

  1. The public can be assured that accredited programs are evaluated & inspected extensively & conform to the expectations of education.
  2. Students can identify & choose programs that meet quality standards of education.
  3. Institutions benefit from the self-evaluation & program improvement provided by the accreditation process & earn credibility.
  4. Any profession benefit from crucial input into the standards established for its future professionals.



What are the benefits of the Accreditation?

  1. Accreditation determines whether an institution is meeting standards of education & provides official recognition by the community.
  2. Accreditation improves enrollment rate by winning trust of students & parents.
  3. Accreditation encourages strategic planning, identifies areas for improvement, & provides substantial information that can be used to support human or financial resource decisions.
  4. Accreditation is extremely significant in recruiting outstanding staff & students.
  5. Accreditation contributes to the assurance that graduates of these programs have proper preparation that meets nationally accepted standards & quality.
  6. Accreditation enhances program credibility & knowledge level of students.
  7. Accreditation helps traditional programs to cope with changes in the fast-developing academic world.
  8. Accreditation self-study & site-visit processes provide opportunities to help faculty members, personnel, & leadership to better understand programs offered by the institution.
  9. Accreditation helps ensure that the institution is a leader in education.
  10. Accreditation promotes self-improvement of educational institutions & program improvement.
  11. Accreditation can be a very strong factor in program retention debates.
  12. Accreditation offers a competitive advantage for programs, students, & careers.
  13. Accreditation may affect the amount of state money that the institution receives.



Steps of educational Accreditation:




  1. Self-assessment: the institution that seeks an accreditation, should perform a self-assessment to its operational processes according to the relevant local or international standards. This can be done by their own staff, or with the help of an external quality consulting agency, like 4Z for Quality.
  2. Application for the accreditation: the institution applies for the accreditation & signs an agreement with the accrediting body.
  3. Eligibility verification of the accreditation: the accrediting body examines documents sent by the institution (proof of compliance to the educational standards set forth by the accreditation body), & a review report is sent back to the institution.
  4. Site-visit: the accrediting body assign reviewers (number of reviewers varies according to the size of the institution & number of programs). Then, a visit schedule is set up in coordination with the institution. Site-visit is executed, in which, interviews are conducted with students & staff. Facilities & equipment are inspected, & documents are examined.
  5. Review report: after the site-visit, reviewers submit an initial report draft, that will be verified by consultants. Then, a copy with improvement recommendations, if any, is sent to the institution.
  6. Accreditation decision: if the institution fulfils the requirement, a final report is issued to recommend accreditation. The accreditation status of the institution is then announced.
  7. Periodic follow-up: the institution prepares an annual report on its status, & efforts made to implement any recommendations of the accrediting body. Subsequently, the accrediting body examines the report, make a site-visit to the institution (if needed), & issues a follow-up report & send it to the institution. This ensures that the institution continue to adhere to high standards after initially earning accreditation.
